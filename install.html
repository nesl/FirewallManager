ipShield gives you richer privacy control over sensor data on Android phones. Right now, many of the common sensors on your phone—along with the information they yield—go unpoliced by the OS's data protection schemes. ipShield takes advantage of Android's virtualized execution environment to give you control over who can access your sensor information and how much they can see.

ipShield…

  • monitors sensors used by the apps on your phone
  • assesses the risks this data can expose you to
  • recommends a course of action to minimize those risks
  • enables you to choose which risks are worth taking, and provides fine-grained control over how sensor data should be passed to each app

How to get it

ipShield consists of two compontents: a background service, and an Android app that allows you to talk to that service. The background service must be installed by patching the operating system kernel. The app may be downloaded or installed via Google Play.
